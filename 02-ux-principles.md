# UX Principles

## Main UX Rule

The user should never have to think.

Every interaction should be obvious, immediate, and low-stakes.

## Desired User Reaction

When the user sees the challenge, they should think:

> That's easy enough.

Not:

> I'll do that later.

## Challenge UX Rules

Every challenge must be:

- immediately doable
- under 60 seconds
- one sentence
- no equipment
- no setup
- no explanation required
- no future scheduling
- not emotionally heavy
- not physically intense

## Interaction Rules

- One primary action: **That counts**
- No forms in the primary flow
- No onboarding required for MVP
- No multi-step completion flow
- No confirmation modal

## Tone

The app voice should be:

- calm
- minimal
- lightly human
- non-judgmental
- not cute
- not motivational
- not themed

## Approved Copy

Headers:

- Today
- One small thing
- Good Enough

Primary button:

- That counts

Completion feedback:

- Nice.
- Good enough.
- Counted.
- That’s enough.

Missed day copy:

- No guilt copy.
- Empty square only.

## Avoid

Do not use:

- "Crush your goals"
- "Keep the streak alive"
- "You failed"
- "Level up"
- "Productivity boost"
- "Optimize your day"
- mascot language
- excessive emojis
- confetti
- loud animations
